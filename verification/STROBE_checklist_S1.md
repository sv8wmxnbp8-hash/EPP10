## Reporting-guideline scope note

The present study is an **ecological cohort-time-arm meta-analysis of aggregate published data** (71 Author × Cohort tuples from 25 independent publications; §2.1 of the manuscript). The STROBE cohort checklist was developed for **primary observational cohort studies with subject-level follow-up**. Several items therefore require adaptation:

- *Setting, Participants, Data sources* (items 5, 6, 8) are reported at the **source-publication level** rather than the recruitment-site level. The unit of analysis is the (Author, cohort) tuple, not the individual subject.
- *Loss to follow-up* (item 12d) is **not applicable**: the analysis does not follow subjects over time. Periprandial trajectories represent cross-sectional meal-test responses from each source study.
- *Confounder adjustment* (items 7, 16) is **not applicable at the subject level**: exposure (cohort membership) is the only covariate modeled, and no individual-level confounders are available. Between-study heterogeneity (caloric protocol, sampling schedule, assay, units, demographics) is acknowledged as expected and is handled by multi-level harmonization (decisions D1–D4; §2.1 and preregistration YAML) rather than by statistical adjustment.
- *Subject-level flow diagram* (item 13c) is **replaced by** cohort-arm-level coverage (Table 1, §3.1) and the cohort normalization map (preregistration YAML + `cohort_normalization_map.csv`).

Notwithstanding these adaptations, **each of the 22 STROBE items is explicitly addressed** as documented in the cross-reference table below. TRIPOD+AI (Collins et al. 2024) is **not applicable** to this manuscript because the analysis is descriptive-inferential, not a multivariable individual-level prediction model (stated explicitly in §1.3 Aim and §6 Reporting Guidelines and Declarations).
